What is Prevent?

The Prevent programme is a key part of the UK's counter-terrorism strategy, known as CONTEST

The core mission of Prevent is stopping people from becoming terrorists or supporting terrorism. The early intervention support provided by Prevent addresses the personal, ideological, and social factors which make people more susceptible to radicalisation.

Signs of radicalisation

The Prevent programme identifies several signs that may indicate someone is being radicalised. These signs can vary, but here are some common indicators:

  • Withdrawal from family and friends, or changing circle of friends
    This could indicate someone is being influenced by others who may have extreme views.
  • Hostility towards others
    Increased aggression and hostility may suggest exposure to extremist ideologies.
  • Talking as if from a script
    Using rehearsed arguments or repeating specific phrases could indicate indoctrination.
  • Being unwilling to discuss their views
    Secrecy about beliefs may indicate awareness that these views are problematic.
  • Increased levels of anger
    Sudden changes in temperament can be a sign of underlying radicalization.
  • Being secretive, particularly around what they are doing on the internet
    Online activity that's kept hidden may involve accessing extremist content.
  • Using extremist terms to exclude people or incite violence
    Using language that promotes hatred or violence against specific groups or individuals.
  • Expressing the values of extremist or terrorist organisations (including political or religious based grievances)
    Advocating for or supporting the ideologies of groups that promote terrorism or extremism, whether political or religious.
  • Supporting violence and terrorism towards other cultures, nationalities, or religions
    Endorsing or justifying acts of violence against people from different cultural, national, or religious backgrounds.
  • Writing or creating artwork that promotes extremist values
    Producing content, such as writings or drawings, that glorifies or promotes extremist ideologies.
  • Talking about being a ‘martyr’
    Expressing a desire to sacrifice oneself for a perceived cause, often associated with extremist or terrorist groups.
  • Possession of extremist literature or other material, or trying to access extremist websites
    Having books, pamphlets, or digital content that supports extremist views, or attempting to visit websites that promote such ideologies.
  • Possession of any material about weapons, explosives, or military training
    Holding information or items related to weaponry, explosives, or combat training, which could indicate preparation for violent acts.

Get in Touch

If you or someone you know are worried about radicalisation please get in touch with a member of the Prevent team in your local area. All contact is confidential
